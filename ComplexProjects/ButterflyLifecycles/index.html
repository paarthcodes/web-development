Stages of a Butterfly

All butterflies start as eggs in the first stage. Eggs are laid attached to a branch or tucked safely on a leaf. Butterfly eggs are very small!

In the second stage, a caterpillar hatches from an egg. A caterpillar looks like a fat worm. It feeds on leaves until it grows big enough. It has two sets of specialized legs to help it grip branches.

In the third stage, the caterpillar creates a chrysalis. A chrysalis usually hangs from a branch. It's basically a tough shell that the caterpillar builds around itself. Then the caterpillar dissolves into a liquid and rebuilds itself as a butterfly!

The fouth and final stage is a butterfly.The butterfly's final form is grown inside the chrysalis. When it's done forming, a butterfly emerges from the chrysalis. A butterfly has a head, thorax, abdomen, and two wings.
